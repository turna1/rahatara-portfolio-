About Me

Hello, I’m Rahatara — an AI researcher with a Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of Ottawa. I specialize in AI-powered Digital Twins for railway safety and healthcare.

My research began with structured EHR data and evolved while working with MCRLab, where I built advanced Digital Twin frameworks integrating machine learning, computer vision, and edge deployment.

In collaboration with the National Research Council Canada (NRC), I published 20+ peer-reviewed papers, with over 500 citations, and presented at top international conferences.

I developed synthetic datasets to address data scarcity — promoting responsible AI by reducing bias, ensuring data integrity, and lowering preparation costs.

I serve on the technical committee for IEEE CTSoc, reviewing research in smart cities, machine learning, and data analysis.

I’ve taught AI and Python, and spoken at GDG, Microsoft Reactor, and ACM Women.

“Those who seek and share knowledge wholeheartedly achieve success through steady growth, consistent progress, and purposeful impact.”

I’m passionate about transforming AI research into ethical, scalable, real-world solutions that create lasting impact.
