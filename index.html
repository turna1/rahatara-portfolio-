About Me

Like energy, data transforms from scarcity to insight, from bias to balance, shaping AI that serves with purpose.

Hello, I’m Rahatara, an AI researcher with a Ph.D. from the University of Ottawa. I specialize in AI-powered Digital Twins for railway safety and healthcare.

My work bridges machine learning, computer vision, and synthetic data to create scalable, real-world systems. Read more

I began with structured EHR data and evolved into working with complex, heterogeneous sources at MCRLab, where I developed advanced Digital Twin frameworks.

In collaboration with National Research Council Canada (NRC), I presented at international conferences and published 20+ papers with 500+ citations.

To address data scarcity, I created synthetic datasets — improving model fairness, data integrity, and reducing preparation costs while promoting responsible AI.

I currently serve as a reviewer for the IEEE CTSoc, and speak regularly at events by Google Developer Group, Microsoft Reactor, and ACM Women.

“Those who seek and share knowledge wholeheartedly achieve success through steady growth, consistent progress, and purposeful impact.”
