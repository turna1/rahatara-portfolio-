About Me

Hello, I’m Rahatara — an AI researcher with a Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of Ottawa. I specialize in AI-powered Digital Twins for railway safety and healthcare.

My research began with structured EHR data and evolved while working with MCRLab, where I handled multimodal data from heterogenous sources and built advanced Digital Twin frameworks integrating generative ai, and computer vision.

In collaboration with the National Research Council Canada (NRC), I contributed to advancing AI-based visual defect inspection for railway components by addressing limitations in the Canadian Pacific Railway dataset. I have published over 20 peer-reviewed papers, accumulated 500+ citations, and presented at leading international conferences.

In my PhD thesis, a synthetic dataset generation pipeline is designed and developed to address data scarcity — promoting responsible AI by reducing bias, ensuring data integrity, and lowering preparation costs.

I serve on the technical committee for IEEE CTSoc, reviewing research in smart cities, machine learning, and data analysis.

I’ve taught AI and Python, and spoken at workshops/conferences organized by tech communities includingk GDG, Microsoft Reactor, and ACM Women.

My professional philosophy is :

“Those who seek and share knowledge continuously, achieve success through steady growth, consistent progress, and purposeful impact.”

I’m passionate about transforming AI research into real-world solutions that create beneficial impact.

Project Highlights

  • Brush of Breath (Submitted to CVPR2025 Generative Art Contest) – An AI-powered generative content generation project that transforms users’ bio-feedback (breathing patterns) into dynamic visual experience (generative artwork) to support breathing excercise.
  • HiMa – A Multimodal Generative AI application designed to assist expecting mothers by leveraging Gemini 2.0 for tasks like item identification from photos and custom podcast generation.
  • DefectTwin – A project that integrates Large Language Models (LLMs) with Digital Twins for railway defect inspection, offering tools for defect simulation and real-time inspection.
  • GenAI-For-Goods – A project focused on utilizing Generative AI for creating synthetic datasets, particularly in scenarios where traditional data collection is challenging.
  • Non-Invasive Anemia Detection – A computer vision-based approach to detect anemia using images of the conjunctiva, offering a non-invasive alternative to traditional blood tests.
