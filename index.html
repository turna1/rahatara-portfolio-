Hello, I’m Rahatara — an AI researcher with a Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of Ottawa, specializing in AI-powered Digital Twins for railway safety and healthcare.

My research journey began with structured electronic health record (EHR) data. During my time at the Multimedia Communications Research Laboratory (MCRLab) , I transitioned to working with complex, heterogeneous datasets from diverse sources. This led me to design scalable Digital Twin frameworks that integrate machine learning, computer vision, and edge AI.

In collaboration with the National Research Council Canada (NRC) , I presented my work at leading international conferences and authored 20+ peer-reviewed publications with 500+ citations.

To address data scarcity in real-world domains, I created synthetic datasets—demonstrating their power to promote responsible AI by reducing bias, improving integrity, and lowering data preparation costs. I believe data, like energy, can be transformed from one form to another.

I currently serve as a technical committee member for the IEEE Consumer Technology Society (CTSoc) , reviewing papers on machine learning, smart cities, and data analytics.

Beyond research, I’ve taught AI and Python, and spoken at global tech communities including Google Developer Group, Microsoft Reactor, and ACM Women.

My passion lies in translating AI research into real-world solutions that are not just intelligent, but ethical, inclusive, and impactful.
